## 🔄 The Big Swap: CBOW vs. Skip-gram

In the last video, you learned that **CBOW** is like a "fill-in-the-blank" game where you give the AI the surrounding words and ask it to guess the missing center word.

**Skip-gram is the exact opposite flip of that coin!** 🪙

> 🕵️‍♂️ **The Skip-gram Analogy:** Think of Skip-gram as a detective given a single clue. You hand the AI **one single center word** (Input), and you force it to predict the entire scene around it—the **surrounding context words** (Outputs).

Using the instructor's example sentence: *"iNeuron company is related to data science"*:

* **CBOW Input:** `["iNeuron", "company", "related", "to"]` ➡️ **Predicts Output:** `"is"`
* **Skip-gram Input:** `"is"` ➡️ **Predicts Outputs:** `["iNeuron", "company", "related", "to"]`

---

## 🧠 Inside the Skip-gram Neural Network Brain

To train a Skip-gram model, we use a single-layer neural network that works like an upscale factory line:

1. **The Input Layer:** You feed in your single center word (like `"is"`) as a basic One-Hot Encoded vector of 0s and 1s.
2. **The Hidden Layer:** The text passes through a hidden bottleneck layer. The number of nodes here matches your target **Dimension Size** (5 in this tutorial, but 300 in Google's real-world model).
3. **The Output Layer (Softmax Power):** The network branches out to try and predict *multiple* words at the same time. It uses a mathematical function called **Softmax**.
* *What Softmax does in simple terms:* It turns raw neural network numbers into clean **percentages/probabilities** (e.g., *"There is an 85% chance the word 'company' is nearby"*). 🎯


4. **The Learning Loop:** The **Loss Function** checks how wrong those percentages were compared to the real sentence, and the **Optimizer** travels backward, tweaking the weight dials until the map becomes perfectly accurate.

## ⚔️ CBOW vs. Skip-gram: Which One Wins?

The instructor dropped an incredibly valuable industry rule of thumb here. Here is how they stack up in the real world:

| Feature | CBOW (Continuous Bag of Words) | Skip-gram |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **The Game** | Predicts **1 Center Word** from Context. | Predicts **Multiple Context Words** from 1 Word. |
| **Best Dataset Size** | 📉 **Small Datasets** (Trains much faster). | 🐘 **Huge Datasets** (Takes longer to train). |
| **Handling Rare Words** | 🙈 Struggles with unusual or rare words. | 🎯 Excellent at capturing context for rare words. |

## 🚀 Two Pro-Tips to Make Your Word2Vec Smarter

If your Word2Vec model's predictions are messy, the instructor gave you two direct levers to pull to improve its accuracy:

* **Lever 1 — Increase the Window Size:** If your window size is 5, you only look 2 words ahead and 2 words behind. If you scale that up, your vector dimensions increase (e.g., up to Google's standard of 300). More dimensions mean the AI can map much more complex language patterns!
* **Lever 2 — Feed it More Data:** The more text the AI reads, the better it becomes. For instance, Google's pre-trained model read **3 billion words** from Google News. Because it read so much, it can take a word like *"cricket"* and instantly spit out a flawless 300-number coordinate map matching it seamlessly to both sports and insects! 🏏🦗
