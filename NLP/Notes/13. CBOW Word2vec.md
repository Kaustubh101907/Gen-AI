## 🛠️ The 3 Deep Learning Prerequisites

Before building Word2Vec, you need to know how a computer learns using a digital brain:

* **ANN (Artificial Neural Network):** Think of this as a digital simulator of a human brain. It takes inputs, passes them through hidden processing channels (layers), and spits out a prediction at the end.
* **Loss Function:** The "Strict Math Teacher." Every time the AI makes a guess, the loss function calculates exactly how wrong that guess was. A high loss score means a terrible guess; a zero loss score means perfection. 📈
* **Optimizer:** The "Mechanic." When the loss function says a guess was wrong, the optimizer steps in, runs backward through the network (**Backpropagation**), and slightly tweaks the internal connections (weights) so the AI gets smarter on the next attempt.

---

## 🎯 What is CBOW (Continuous Bag of Words)?

**CBOW** is one of the two core architectures used to train Google's famous **Word2Vec** model.

> 🕵️‍♂️ **The Core Concept:** CBOW treats language like a "fill-in-the-blank" guessing game. You give the AI the surrounding **context words**, and force it to predict the single **missing target word** in the center.

Let's trace how the AI uses this game to learn from the instructor's example sentence:
*"iNeuron company is related to data science"* (7 unique words in our vocabulary).

---

## 🪟 Step 1: The Sliding Window Game

To create a supervised training dataset out of a flat sentence, we use a moving **Window Size** (let's use an odd number like **5** so there is a perfect center word).

We slide this window down our text step-by-step to automatically split our text into inputs and outputs:

### Window Position 1:

* **The 5 Words Captured:** `["iNeuron", "company", "is", "related", "to"]`
* **Target Output Word (The Center):** `"is"`
* **Input Context Words (The Surrounding):** `["iNeuron", "company"]` and `["related", "to"]`

### Window Position 2 (Slide right by one word):

* **The 5 Words Captured:** `["company", "is", "related", "to", "data"]`
* **Target Output Word (The Center):** `"related"`
* **Input Context Words (The Surrounding):** `["company", "is"]` and `["to", "data"]`

By doing this across millions of sentences, the AI gets a massive list of inputs (context) and targets (answers) to practice on.

---

## 🏗️ Step 2: The Word2Vec Training Factory Layout

To train the model, we use a simple 3-layer neural network layout:

### 1. The Input Layer (The Words Go In)

We cannot pass text into an ANN directly. We take our 4 context words and convert them into basic **One-Hot Encoded vectors** (since our vocabulary size is 7, each word gets a list of seven 1s and 0s) just to push them through the front door.

### 2. The Hidden Layer (The Bottleneck)

This is where the magic happens! The hidden layer acts like a compression chamber. The number of nodes in this layer determines your final vector's size (your **Dimension Size**).

* In the video's example, the instructor sets this to **5**.
* In Google's real-world model, they set this hidden layer to **300**.

### 3. The Output Layer (The Guess)

The network takes the compressed information from the hidden layer and attempts to light up the correct slot for the single missing target word in your vocabulary list.

---

## 🔄 Step 3: The Learning Loop (Forward & Backward)

1. **The Guess:** You feed the network `["iNeuron", "company", "related", "to"]`. It runs through the layers and guesses a word (`y_hat`).
2. **The Correction:** The **Loss Function** measures how close the guess was to the real answer (`"is"`).
3. **The Upgrade:** The **Optimizer** shoots that error score backward through the network, shifting the numeric connection strengths (weights) on all the connecting lines.

---

## 👑 The Ultimate Plot Twist: Harvesting the Weights

Here is the most important thing to remember about Word2Vec: **We don't actually care about the network's final guesses!** Once the network becomes incredibly good at guessing the missing words, we stop training and completely **throw away the output layer**.

The final, highly-optimized connection weights inside the **Hidden Layer** become our permanent **Word2Vec Embeddings**. Because those numeric connections were adjusted over and over again based on context, those final 5 numbers (or 300 numbers for Google) represent the true, deep semantic meaning of the word! 🎉
